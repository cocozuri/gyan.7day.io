Introduction



    Here's a quick introduction to this pocketbook from the 7-Days Course by Brahma Kumaris.

    In today's educational institutions, students primarily focus on secular subjects like language, history, geography, science, civics, laws, customs, and traditions. While these subjects offer knowledge and can lead to prestigious careers, they do not guarantee comprehensive and lasting happiness. This is where spiritual education comes into play, as it aims to guide individuals towards a higher purpose and a more fulfilling existence.

    The ultimate goal of spiritual education is to empower people to attain divinity, much like the revered deities Shri Narayana and Shri Lakshmi, who epitomize all forms of happiness. It encourages individuals to strive for liberation from suffering and to cultivate divine qualities within themselves. By emphasizing character development and adherence to divine principles, spiritual education helps individuals overcome negative impulses such as lust, anger, greed, attachment, and arrogance.

    Through practical training, individuals learn to purify their thoughts, words, and actions, ultimately achieving a state of complete purity. This transformative process enables them to rise above their limitations and attain a pinnacle of spiritual enlightenment, where true and lasting happiness can be found. Thus, spiritual education serves as a powerful tool for personal growth, self-improvement, and the attainment of a more meaningful and fulfilling life.
